# Professional Summary — Evidence-Centered AI Systems

I am an independent AI systems builder focused on evidence discipline, provenance, agent reliability, and human-centered AI governance. My operating principle is simple: uncertainty should never be silently converted into authority.

My work under CP8 / ASIN-HHC, Cathedral-OS, and WEAVER-style frameworks focuses on systems that track where claims come from, what evidence supports them, how claims should be classified, and whether results can be reproduced. The work includes evidence tiers, receipt-bound claims, audit trails, replay verification, promotion gates, multi-agent review loops, provenance manifests, and UI-first tooling for making complex AI workflows understandable.

My DeepSpec review work examined speculative decoding architecture, distributed training, evaluation design, checkpoint safety, configuration validation, and production-readiness concerns. That review identified both strengths and concrete risks, including unsafe checkpoint deserialization, assertion-based runtime validation, and the need for stronger operational reliability controls.

The CP8 / CP9 work explores structured geometric data, symbolic workflows, and verification layers as a research direction for studying pattern recognition, multi-modal reasoning, and constraint-aware AI systems. This is best presented as independent research and systems design, not as settled science or a finished production platform.

The practical objective is to build AI workflows that are inspectable, accountable, reproducible, accessible, and useful under real-world constraints.
